Introduction

The Machine's Playbook

This book is about understanding a machine‚Äînot the metal and circuits kind, but the psychological kind. The kind that knows exactly which buttons to press in your brain, which rewards to dangle just out of reach, and which moments of hope to manufacture to keep you coming back. The kind of machine that cost me over $200,000 before I finally understood what I was up against.

I'm Dave, and I spent years feeding pokies machines across Australia. Not because I was weak, or foolish, or lacking willpower. I lost that money because I was outmatched. The people who designed these machines understood human psychology better than I understood myself. They built a system that exploited vulnerabilities I didn't even know I had.

But here's what changed everything: I stopped trying to beat the machine at its own game. Instead, I learned to think like the people who built it. I studied the psychology, the design principles, the deliberate manipulation tactics. And once I understood the playbook‚Äîonce I could see the manipulation for what it was‚Äîthe spell broke.

This book is that playbook.

My Story: $200,000 in Lessons

It started in 2001. I was working as a chef with a two-hour break between shifts. The venue had a pokies lounge. Fifty dollars, one machine, two hours to kill. Simple enough.

Except it was never simple. Within weeks, I had a "favourite" machine. Within months, I was losing more than I earned. Within years, I'd convinced myself I understood the patterns, that I could predict the payouts, that I was different from all the other people sitting at those machines with the same desperate hope in their eyes.

I hit a breaking point and stopped. For a while. But like most people who think they've beaten an addiction through willpower alone, I came back. This time, I watched a friend bet $5 per spin and win $600 in minutes. That was all it took. I upped my bets from $1 to $5, convinced that bigger risks meant bigger rewards.

They didn't.

When I finally sat down and calculated my total losses‚Äîover $200,000‚ÄîI realised something crucial: I hadn't been playing against a machine. I'd been playing against a system designed by psychologists, data analysts, and addiction specialists who understood human behaviour far better than I did. They'd built a trap, and I'd walked into it willingly, repeatedly, for years.

The real cost wasn't just the money. It was the time, the relationships, the erosion of control over my own life. It was the belief that I was somehow special, that I could beat a system specifically designed to be unbeatable.

But I learned something else, too: understanding the system changes everything.

The Approach: Designing The Machine

Here's what we're going to do together. Throughout this book, Dave and I will design the most psychologically manipulative pokies machine imaginable. We'll walk through every decision a designer would make to maximise addiction:

  • How to hijack your dopamine system with intermittent rewards
  • How to create the illusion of control over random outcomes
  • How to use sound and light to synchronise with your heartbeat
  • How to exploit cultural identity and childhood nostalgia
  • How to build artificial communities that trap you in social obligation
  • How to escalate your spending through pride and loss-chasing
  • How to use loyalty programs to create sunk-cost fallacies
  • How to deploy advanced features that make resistance nearly impossible

Why design something so manipulative? Because every element we'll design is already out there. The machines in your local pub or casino use these exact tactics. What we're doing is exposing the playbook. Once you see the manipulation laid bare‚Äîonce you understand the designer's intent behind every flashing light and carefully timed payout‚Äîthe illusion loses its power.

Think of it like learning how a magic trick works. Once you see the sleight of hand, the trapdoor, the hidden mirror‚Äîthe trick stops working. That's what we're doing here. We're showing you the trapdoors.

A Warning About Discomfort

Some of what you're about to read will feel wrong. When Dave and I discuss how to make machines more addictive, how to exploit psychological vulnerabilities, how to design features that keep people spending beyond their means‚Äîit's meant to disturb you.

This is intentional. This is how you inoculate yourself against manipulation. By seeing it clearly, by understanding the intent, you become immune to it.

If you feel uncomfortable reading our design conversations, that's your humanity responding correctly to calculated exploitation. Don't look away. Lean into that discomfort. It's the first step toward freedom.

The Victory Ahead

By the end of this book, you'll understand something crucial: you're not weak. You're not broken. You're not lacking willpower.

You were up against a system designed by experts specifically to defeat willpower. They had psychologists, millions in research funding, decades of behavioural data, and regulatory capture on their side. You had hope and determination.

That was never a fair fight.

But now you'll have something else: knowledge. Complete visibility into their playbook. Understanding of every tactic they use to keep you hooked. And most importantly, a clear path to walk away and stay away.

The victory isn't in beating the machine at its own game. The victory is in refusing to play.

Let's begin.
