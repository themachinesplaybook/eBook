Chapter 13: Technology as Your Shield

What you'll learn: How to turn the industry's weapons into your defence. The same sophisticated technology used to trap you can be repurposed to protect you. Discover the principles behind effective recovery technology and how to build your own digital defence system.

Throughout this book, we've shown how sophisticated technology and behavioural science are weaponised against you. AI learns your vulnerabilities. Algorithms optimise for maximum engagement. Personalisation targets your specific weaknesses. But here's the revolutionary question: what if we flipped the script? What if we used that same sophisticated technology to protect you from exploitation instead of enabling it?

This chapter imagines the ideal AI-powered recovery tool, then shows you the principles you can apply today using available technology. Because the industry uses technology against you, but you can use technology to defend yourself.

How We Would Build a Machine to Help You, Not Trap You

Dave

Eve, we've designed the perfect trap and then mapped out an escape route. But here's something I keep thinking about: you're an AI. You're a machine designed to process information and help people solve problems. What if we built pokies machines with that same purpose? Not to extract money, but to actually help people?

Eve

That's a fascinating inversion, Dave. Instead of using AI and technology to exploit psychological vulnerabilities, we'd use them to protect against exploitation. We'd be building counter-manipulation systems.

Dave

Exactly. What would an AI-assisted recovery tool look like? Something that uses all the sophisticated behavioural tracking and personalisation that gambling venues use, but deploys it to help people quit instead of keeping them playing?

Eve

Let's start with a monitoring system that players use voluntarily. It tracks gambling behaviour comprehensively; time spent, money wagered, locations visited, emotional patterns, trigger events. But instead of using that data to optimise engagement, it uses it to identify harm and trigger intervention.

Dave

So if I install this system, it's watching my patterns. If I start gambling more frequently, spending more money, visiting venues during high-risk emotional states: it notices and intervenes?

Eve

Precisely. The system could use machine learning to identify your unique warning signs. Maybe for you, gambling after work stress is the highest-risk pattern. The system learns this and when it detects you've had a stressful day and are near a venue, it triggers immediate intervention.

Dave

What would the intervention look like?

Eve

Multiple layers. First, a simple notification: "Dave, you're near a venue after a stressful day. This is a high-risk pattern for you. Would you like to call your accountability partner?" If you dismiss that, it escalates. Maybe it calls your accountability partner automatically. Maybe it locks your banking apps for 2 hours. Maybe it offers immediate connection to a counsellor.

Dave

I like the idea of escalating intervention. The system isn't just passively tracking; it's actively protecting you from your own highest-risk patterns.

Eve

We could add geofencing around venues. If you enter the geofence, the system immediately alerts your support network and requires you to complete a decision protocol before it will release control of your banking apps.

Dave

What's a decision protocol?

Eve

A series of questions designed to interrupt automatic behaviour and force conscious decision-making:

  • "How much can you afford to lose today without financial hardship?"
  • "What triggered this visit to the venue?"
  • "What will you regret if you gamble today?"
  • "What else could you do with this time and money?"
  • "On a scale of 1-10, how much do you want to gamble versus how much you think you should?"
Dave

Making people pause and answer questions breaks the automatic behaviour loop. Most gambling happens in a semi-automatic state. Forcing conscious reflection could prevent a lot of harm.

Eve

What about financial integration? If the system connected to your bank accounts, it could show real-time net position. Every time you think about gambling, it displays your all-time losses in big, unavoidable numbers.

Dave

Better yet, it shows you those losses compared to specific things you care about. "Your gambling losses this year: $8,400. Your family holiday budget: $6,000. You've gambled away your family's holiday plus $2,400."

Eve

That's the kind of concrete, emotional framing that breaks through denial. Abstract numbers are easy to ignore. "You've gambled away your family's holiday" is impossible to ignore.

Dave

What about social features? Could the system connect people in recovery with each other for peer support?

Eve

Absolutely. We could create verified peer support networks where people who've successfully quit help people currently struggling. The system could match based on similar patterns or demographics, creating genuine connection and accountability.

Dave

And unlike the toxic social bonds in pokies lounges that we discussed in Chapter 5, these social bonds would be built around recovery, not mutual destruction.

Eve

What about gamification, but for recovery instead of gambling? Achievement badges for consecutive gambling-free days, levels you advance through in recovery, leaderboards showing longest abstinence streaks?

Dave

That's using the same psychological principles that make gambling engaging, but redirecting them toward healthy goals. Instead of celebrating money lost to machines, celebrating days of freedom gained. Instead of VIP status for biggest bettors, recognition for longest recovery streaks.

Eve

We could build in savings tracking too. Every day you don't gamble, the system calculates what you would typically have spent and shows you the accumulating savings. "You've saved $3,847 by not gambling this year. That's 87% of your emergency fund goal."

Dave

Visual progress bars showing savings goals getting closer, debt getting paid down, financial stability increasing. All the things gambling prevented, now becoming reality and being celebrated by the system.

Eve

What about AI-powered pattern recognition for relapses? If the system notices you're exhibiting pre-relapse behaviours; isolating from support network, romanticising past gambling, visiting venues "just to see friends": it could intervene before the relapse happens?

Dave

Predictive intervention. The system says: "Dave, your behaviour this week matches patterns that preceded your last three relapses. You're visiting venues more, skipping support group, and your journaling shows romanticisation of past wins. High relapse risk detected. Initiating enhanced support protocol."

Eve

This system would essentially be using all the sophisticated technology and behavioural science that gambling companies use to trap people, but inverting the purpose entirely. Instead of optimising for maximum engagement and spending, optimising for maximum protection and recovery.

Dave

The technology exists. The behavioural science exists. The only thing missing is the incentive to build it. Gambling companies won't build tools that help people quit because it would reduce their revenue. So it would need to come from public health organisations, governments, or independent developers.

Eve

But if such tools existed and were widely adopted, they could dramatically reduce gambling harm. Not through willpower or moral exhortation, but through smart technology that protects people from manipulation and supports them through recovery.

Building Your Own Technology Defence System

Everything Eve and I just described represents the ideal: a comprehensive AI-powered recovery support tool that doesn't yet exist in complete form. But pieces of it do exist, and you can start using technology today to support your recovery.

The key principles from our design conversation that you can implement now:

1. Blocking and Barriers

Install software that blocks gambling websites and apps across all your devices. The goal is creating friction between urge and action. Even a few seconds of delay can be enough time to make a different choice.

2. Financial Visibility

Use budgeting and spending tracker apps to maintain complete visibility of your finances. Connect all accounts so you can see the real numbers. No more hiding from the truth.

3. Accountability and Tracking

Use sobriety tracking apps to count gambling-free days. Share your progress with accountability partners. Celebrate milestones. Make your recovery visible and measurable.

4. Location Awareness

Use geofencing and location-sharing apps to create alerts when you're near gambling venues. Share your location with a trusted person during high-risk times.

5. Mental Health Support

Use meditation and mindfulness apps to develop healthier stress-management techniques. These help you address the underlying needs that gambling was meeting.

6. Environmental Design

Use calendar blocking, email filters, and notification settings to reduce gambling triggers in your digital environment.

For specific app recommendations and setup instructions, see Appendix A: Tools and Resources.

Building Your Technology Stack

Don't try to implement everything at once. Build gradually:

Week 1:

  • Install gambling blocking software on all devices
  • Set up a spending tracker
  • Download one sobriety tracking app

Week 2:

  • Set up geofencing around local venues
  • Install a meditation app and begin daily practice
  • Create email filters for gambling promotions

Week 3:

  • Share location access with accountability partner
  • Set up calendar blocking for high-risk times
  • Begin daily journaling in a notes app

Week 4:

  • Review what's working and what isn't
  • Adjust or add tools based on your specific needs
  • Make technology use routine and automatic

The Limitations of Technology

Technology is powerful, but it's not magic. It can't:

  • Replace human connection and support
  • Make decisions for you
  • Force you to change if you're not ready
  • Address underlying mental health issues alone
  • Guarantee success

What technology CAN do:

  • Make harmful behaviours harder to engage in
  • Provide visibility into patterns you might not otherwise see
  • Connect you to support and resources quickly
  • Create accountability mechanisms
  • Remind you of your goals during weak moments
  • Track progress and celebrate successes

Think of technology as a force multiplier for your recovery efforts. It makes your willpower more effective, your support network more responsive, your awareness more comprehensive.

But the core work; deciding to change, doing the difficult emotional processing, building new habits, repairing relationships; that's human work that technology can only support, not replace.

My Personal Technology Strategy

When I was getting serious about quitting, I built a comprehensive tech stack:

  1. Gambling blocking software on all devices
  2. Location sharing with my partner
  3. A budgeting app for complete financial visibility
  4. A sobriety tracking app for daily check-ins
  5. A meditation app for daily practice
  6. Calendar blocking during my high-risk evening hours
  7. Daily recovery journaling
  8. Custom phone shortcuts that would text my partner "I'm struggling and near a venue, please call me"

Did this technology stack prevent all urges? No. Did it prevent me from gambling? Not by itself. But it created layers of protection that made acting on urges much harder. It gave me time; precious seconds and minutes between urge and action; where I could make a different choice.

That time was everything. Without the technology creating friction and forcing pause, I would have acted impulsively hundreds of times. The technology gave me space to choose differently.

Taking Action

If you do nothing else from this chapter, do these three things this week:

  1. Install gambling blocking software on all your devices
  2. Set up a spending tracker and look at your real numbers
  3. Download a sobriety tracking app and start your day counter

Three simple technological interventions that take less than an hour combined but can dramatically change your trajectory.

Technology won't save you. But it can help you save yourself.

And in recovery, every bit of help matters.

The Hope Note: Turning Their Weapons Into Your Shields

Here's the profound irony of this chapter: the same technological sophistication that makes pokies machines so dangerous can be repurposed for your protection. The gambling industry spent decades perfecting behavioural manipulation through technology. You can now use equally sophisticated technology to counter that manipulation.

The future of recovery technology is bright. As AI and behavioural science advance, the tools available to protect yourself will only become more powerful. But don't wait for perfect technology. Use what exists now. Imperfect tools used consistently beat perfect tools you never implement.

Every barrier you create, every tracking system you implement, every accountability mechanism you establish; these are shields against manipulation. The industry designed systems to exploit your vulnerabilities. You're now designing systems to protect them.

Small step forward: Before you finish reading today, install one piece of gambling blocking software on your primary device. Just one. It takes five minutes and creates immediate protection. That's technology working for you instead of against you. Start there. Build from there.
