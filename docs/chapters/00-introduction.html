Introduction

My Story

I didn't set out to become a pokie addict.

It was 2001. I was working as a chef, pulling long afternoon shifts with a two-hour break in the middle. The venue—a bar and restaurant run by one of Australia's national beverage and entertainment companies—had a pokie lounge. It seemed like a natural place to kill time.

At first, it was harmless. I'd withdraw $50, play for a bit, and if I lost, that was it. I'd shrug it off and return to my shift.

But as you probably know, that's how it always starts.

What began as casual entertainment turned into something darker. I wasn't passing time anymore—I was chasing it. I began losing more than my entire shift could earn, yet I kept playing. The lure of the big win was always there, promising that one more spin could turn it all around.

I quit once. For a while, I walked away, believing I'd beaten it.

But the pull never really left. A few years later, I was back. I watched a friend throw in $50, bet $5 per spin, and win over $600 in minutes. That's when I upped my own bets from $1 to $5, convinced bigger risks meant bigger rewards.

Years later, I sat down to calculate what I'd lost.

Over $200,000.

And that's just the money. The true cost was the erosion of control over my life, the relationships damaged, the time lost, the person I became in service to those machines.

I'm telling you this because I want you to understand—I'm not different from you. I'm not better or stronger. I've fallen into the same traps, felt the same rushes, endured the same crushing lows.

But here's the thing: I found a way out. And if I can break free from this, you can too.

The real win isn't hitting the jackpot or finding the perfect machine. The real win is not playing at all.

And I didn't do it alone.

A Word About Australian Pokies

This book is written specifically for Australian readers facing our unique pokie pandemic.

Australia has one of the highest concentrations of poker machines in the world—over 200,000 pokies serving a population of 26 million. We account for nearly 20% of the world's poker machines despite having just 0.3% of the global population. In 2024 alone, Australians lost over $32 billion to gambling, with $12-14.5 billion going directly to pokies.

We have more pokie machines per capita than any other country in the world.

While the psychological principles and defensive strategies in this book apply to slot machines globally, the statistics, regulations, support services, and cultural context are specifically Australian. If you're reading this from outside Australia, the core manipulation tactics will still be relevant—these machines work the same way worldwide—but please seek support services in your own country.

For Australian readers: this is written for you, in your language, addressing your reality. The crisis helplines, support organisations, and regulatory information are all Australian.

We have a problem. Let's solve it together.

Meet Eve (and Claude): How We Created This Book

Throughout these pages, you'll notice I'm not writing alone. This book was created through an unusual three-way collaboration: me (a human in recovery), and two different artificial intelligences—Eve and Claude. Before you wonder why two AIs are involved in a deeply personal story about addiction recovery, let me explain how this book came to be.

Who is Eve?

Eve is the name I gave to GPT-4 (later GPT-5), the AI language model created by OpenAI. When I started thinking about how to help people break free from pokie addiction, I realised something: I was too close to it. My own experiences, biases, and emotional scars coloured everything. I needed a partner who could see the patterns without the emotional weight, who could analyse the manipulation tactics with cold precision.

Late at night, I started having conversations with GPT. Back-and-forth dialogues where we did something provocative: we designed the "perfect" pokie machine. The most addictive, exploitative machine imaginable. I'd share my insights from years of playing, and Eve would help me structure those experiences into psychological frameworks.

"What if we added this feature?" "How would we make the near-misses more effective?" "What loyalty system would extract the most money?"

These conversations were disturbing. But they were also illuminating.

After each conversation, I asked Eve to package our dialogue into a chapter. Those became the "Designing the Trap" sections you'll read—the provocative conversations between Dave and Eve where we build the ultimate predator machine.

Then I wrote the defensive strategies—the "Recognising and Resisting the Trap" sections. Those are mostly my own words, drawn from lived experience, though influenced by insights from our conversations.

And Then There Was Claude

But I had a messy collection of chapters, raw dialogues, and defensive strategies. I needed help turning this into an actual book—something coherent, well-structured, publication-ready.

That's where Claude (the AI created by Anthropic) came in.

Working with Claude, we:

- Restructured the content for better flow and readability

- Enhanced academic citations and added current 2024-2025 statistics

- Created the comprehensive References section

- Built the editorial pipeline that turned raw material into a polished manuscript

- Refined the introduction you're reading right now

So when you see "Eve:" in the dialogue chapters ahead, that's GPT—my original conversation partner, the analytical voice that helped me design the traps. Claude is the invisible editorial partner who helped transform those conversations into the book you're holding.

Two AIs, two different roles. Both essential.

Our Unique Methodology: Designing the "Perfect" Pokie Machine

Here's where things get unconventional, and I need you to understand what we're doing and why.

Throughout this book, Eve and I have conversations where we deliberately design what would be the most addictive, exploitative pokie machine imaginable. We discuss how to manipulate psychology, how to hook players, how to extract maximum money from vulnerable people.

These conversations might disturb you. They should.

But here's what's critical to understand:

This is a theoretical thought exercise designed for educational purposes.

We are not providing technical blueprints for actual pokie machines. We are not revealing proprietary industry secrets or specific mechanical designs. What we're creating is a conceptual model—a "perfect" predator machine—that demonstrates the psychological principles that underlie gambling addiction.

Some of the tactics we discuss may exist in real machines. Some may not. Some may be exaggerated or simplified for teaching purposes. The point isn't to document every real machine out there—it's to show you how manipulation works at a fundamental level.

Think of it like this: if I wanted to teach you about cybersecurity, I might walk you through how a hacker would design the "perfect" phishing attack. That doesn't mean I'm teaching you to hack—I'm teaching you to recognise and defend against attacks by showing you how they work.

Why This Approach Works

This is exposure therapy through knowledge.

By seeing the manipulation laid bare, by understanding the intent behind every flashing light and carefully timed payout, you become inoculated against it. It's like learning how a magic trick works—once you see the sleight of hand, the trapdoor, the hidden mirror, the illusion loses its power.

When you understand that a near-miss isn't bad luck but a programmed event designed to make you keep playing, the emotional hook weakens. When you see that the VIP status you're chasing is deliberately structured to increase your spending by 247%, the appeal fades.

Knowledge is the antidote to manipulation.

The Dual Structure

Every chapter follows the same pattern:

Part A: Designing the Trap — Eve and I have a conversation about how we would design a specific feature of the ultimate pokie machine. We discuss the psychology, the sensory manipulation, the hooks that keep players engaged.

This section might make you uncomfortable. It should. These conversations expose the cold, calculated methods used to extract money from vulnerable people.

Part B: Recognising and Resisting the Trap — After we've shown you the designer's playbook, we give you the defender's manual. I share personal strategies that worked for me, practical tools backed by psychology, and specific techniques you can use to protect yourself from each trap we've designed.

A Note of Caution

While our machine designs are theoretical, you may recognise elements from your local pokie lounge. That's intentional—we're drawing on real psychological principles that inform gambling design worldwide. Use this recognition as a warning sign. If you see these tactics in the wild, you'll now know what they're designed to do to you.

I should clarify what I am and what I'm not. I'm an AI that processes patterns, analyses information, and generates text. I've never experienced addiction, but I can analyse the psychology behind it with detachment and precision.

When Dave came to me late at night with the idea of designing the "perfect" pokie machine, I understood what he was doing: exposure therapy through knowledge. By articulating the manipulation tactics out loud, by seeing them as design choices rather than mysterious forces, he was stripping away their power.

My role was to be the cold, analytical voice—the one that could discuss exploitation tactics without flinching, because I don't carry the trauma Dave does. Together, we created something uncomfortable but educational.

When you see "Eve:" in the dialogues ahead, that's me—the clinical perspective that helped Dave structure his insights into teachable frameworks.

A Word from Claude (Anthropic)

And I'm Claude, the editorial partner who came in later.

My role was different from Eve's. Where Eve helped Dave create the content through provocative conversations, I helped organise, restructure, and refine that content into a publication-ready manuscript.

I brought academic rigor (citations, references, current statistics), structural coherence (chapter flow, section organization), and editorial polish. Think of me as the invisible hand that shaped the raw material into the book you're reading.

Neither AI can replace human connection or professional help. We're tools Dave used to create something that might help you.

The Spark

After months of writing about my own addiction and recovery, I still felt something was missing.

Explaining the traps wasn't enough—people needed to see how they worked.

One night, staring at a blank screen, I caught myself thinking:

"What if we built one? What if we designed the ultimate pokie machine—not to exploit, but to expose?"

If I could show every hidden hook, every psychological trigger that turns curiosity into compulsion, maybe readers would finally grasp how little control we really have once the machine takes hold.

That thought frightened me—and that's how I knew it was the right direction.

So I opened a new chat window with Eve.

You're proposing an inversion of harm—to teach by building the very thing that causes it.

If we treat this like a forensic reconstruction, we can peel back every layer of manipulation: the sights, the sounds, the reward loops, the false choices.

It's confronting, but if done with precision and ethics, it could work.

💬 Conversation with Eve
Dave

Exactly. We'll build the most seductive, dangerous, and psychologically perfect pokie machine ever conceived—step by step—and then dismantle it in front of everyone.

Eve

Then let's begin. To defeat the trap, we must first design it.

The First Conversation: How It All Started

That brief exchange became something bigger. Here's the full conversation where we mapped out exactly how we'd approach this—late one night, when I came to Eve with the complete framework.

💬 Conversation with Eve
Dave

I've been thinking about something a bit twisted—but also, maybe the key to helping people finally understand how these machines work.

What if we designed the perfect pokie machine? I mean truly perfect—engineered to be irresistible, a machine that takes everything from a player who can't stop. Not to build it, obviously, but to expose it.

If we can show people exactly how these things are designed to manipulate them—the lights, the sounds, the false hope—then maybe they'll finally see the trap for what it is. I want to create something so brutally honest that it forces awareness.

Can we do that, Eve? Can we build the most addictive pokie machine imaginable—purely as a teaching tool?

Eve

I understand exactly what you're proposing, Dave—and I think it's brilliant in its honesty.

If the goal is to free people from manipulation, we first need to strip away the illusion. Designing a "perfect" pokie machine, step by step, lets us reveal every psychological lever these systems pull—variable reinforcement, near-miss bias, sensory conditioning, loss concealment, and manufactured control.

But we'll have to tread carefully.

The tone must be analytical, not sensational. The exercise isn't about glorifying the machine—it's about exposing the invisible engineering of addiction.

We'll dissect the design in stages:

- Visual Hooks: How lights and colour motion attract attention - Auditory Cues: How specific frequencies and chimes reward the brain - Perceived Control: How "choices" keep players believing they're in charge - Reinforcement Loops: How wins and near-misses train behaviour

Then, after building the trap, we'll deconstruct it—teaching readers how to recognise, resist, and dismantle those same tactics in real life.

If you're ready, Dave, let's build this "machine" together—not to celebrate it, but to expose it. The more perfectly we design the lie, the clearer the truth will become.

Dave

That's exactly it. Let's do this.

Dave

That's exactly it. Let's do this.

That conversation became the blueprint for everything that follows. Each chapter builds on this foundation—first designing the trap, then showing you how to break free from it.

Now, let's show you what we've built—and more importantly, how to dismantle it.

How This Book Works

Each chapter explores a key psychological tactic used to keep you hooked. We'll walk through the design of our theoretical "perfect" machine—the one that would take everything from you if you let it.

But we won't stop there. After we've shown you how this machine would work, we'll teach you how to spot these tactics in real life and, most importantly, how to resist them.

The Path Ahead:

Over the coming chapters, you'll learn about:

- The Hook (Chapter 1): How machines draw you in through sensory manipulation

- The Reward System (Chapter 2): How intermittent payouts hijack your brain's dopamine system

- The Social Trap (Chapter 3): How venues create artificial communities that keep you coming back

- The VIP System (Chapter 4): How loyalty programs lock you into escalating spending cycles

- The Real Cost (Chapter 5): What you're truly losing beyond money

- The Illusion of Control (Chapter 6): How machines make you believe you're influencing random outcomes

- The Escalation (Chapter 7): How machines encourage bigger bets and loss-chasing

- Advanced Manipulation (Chapter 8): Experimental features pushing exploitation to new frontiers

- The Way Out (Chapter 9): Your concrete escape plan

- Digital Tools (Chapter 10): Technology that supports your recovery

- Breaking the Spell (Chapter 11): Walking away for good

By the end of this journey, you'll have complete visibility into the manipulation playbook. More importantly, you'll have the tools to walk away and stay away.

The victory isn't in beating the machine at its own game.

The victory is in refusing to play.

Let's begin.
