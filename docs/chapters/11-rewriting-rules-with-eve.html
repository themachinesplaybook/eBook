Leveraging Technology for Self-Control: Techniques to Break the Addiction

Everything Eve and I described represents the ideal app‚Äîa comprehensive AI-powered recovery support tool that doesn't yet exist in complete form.

But pieces of it do exist, and you can start using technology today to support your recovery. Here's what's available now and how to use it effectively.

Currently Available Tools

Gambling Blocking Software:

Gamban (www.gamban.com)

  • Blocks all gambling sites and apps across all devices
  • Can't be easily bypassed or uninstalled
  • Costs about $25/year
  • Available for computers, phones, tablets
  • Covers thousands of gambling sites internationally

BetBlocker (betblocker.org)

  • Free, open-source gambling blocking
  • Blocks websites and apps
  • Less comprehensive than Gamban but free
  • Good option if cost is a barrier

Cold Turkey (getcoldturkey.com)

  • General purpose website and app blocker
  • You create custom blocklists including gambling sites
  • Can set schedules (block gambling sites during high-risk hours)
  • Can lock blocks so they can't be bypassed during weak moments

How to use them effectively:

  • Install on ALL devices, not your primary phone
  • Set them up during a moment of clarity, not during urges
  • Tell your accountability partner when you've installed them
  • Don't rely on them alone‚Äîthey're one layer of protection, not a complete solution
  • Use multiple blockers together for redundancy

Financial Control Apps

PocketGuard (pocketguard.com)

  • Tracks all spending automatically
  • Shows how much you can safely spend
  • Identifies spending patterns including gambling
  • Helps you stick to budgets

YNAB (You Need A Budget) (youneedabudget.com)

  • Comprehensive budgeting system
  • Makes you assign every dollar a job
  • Harder to justify gambling when you've explicitly allocated money elsewhere
  • Has educational content about financial decision-making

MoneyBrilliant (moneybrilliant.com.au)

  • Australian-specific financial tracking
  • Categorizes spending automatically
  • Shows gambling spending clearly
  • Free basic version available

How to use them effectively:

  • Connect all your accounts for complete visibility
  • Check the app before making any significant financial decision
  • Set up alerts for unusual spending patterns
  • Share access with your accountability partner if you're comfortable
  • Use the data to calculate your true cost of gambling

Accountability and Support Apps

I Am Sober (iamsober.com)

  • Track days since last gambled
  • Daily pledges and motivational messages
  • Community support features
  • Progress milestones and achievements
  • Can track multiple addictions/habits simultaneously

Nomo (nomoapp.org)

  • Sobriety clock showing time abstinent
  • Chip system based on recovery milestones
  • Private or social options
  • Daily accountability check-ins

AccountaBuddy

  • Pairs you with accountability partners
  • Scheduled check-ins via text
  • Track habits and goals together
  • Social accountability features

How to use them effectively:

  • Update them daily, even when (especially when) you don't want to
  • Share your milestones with support network
  • Use the community features to connect with others in recovery
  • Set up daily reminders to check in
  • Celebrate milestones meaningfully

Meditation and Mental Health Apps

Headspace (headspace.com)

  • Guided meditations for various purposes
  • Specific content for managing cravings and urges
  • Sleepcasts for better sleep
  • Mindfulness exercises for daily life

Calm (calm.com)

  • Meditation and relaxation
  • Sleep stories and music
  • Breathing exercises for stress management
  • Mental health resources

Smiling Mind (smilingmind.com.au)

  • Australian mindfulness app
  • Completely free
  • Programs for stress, sleep, relationships
  • Age-appropriate content for whole family

How to use them effectively:

  • Use them preventatively, not during crisis
  • Start with 5 minutes daily
  • Build a consistent practise during early recovery
  • Use the breathing exercises when urges hit
  • Make mindfulness part of your daily routine

Location and Geofencing Tools

Life360 (life360.com)

  • Family location sharing
  • Set up geofences around gambling venues
  • Alerts when you enter high-risk areas
  • Your support person can see if you're near venues

Google Maps Custom Lists

  • Mark all gambling venues in your area
  • Create custom routes that avoid them
  • Set up alerts when approaching marked areas

How to use them effectively:

  • Be honest about all the venues you visit
  • Give a support person access to your location during high-risk times
  • Use them to plan routes that avoid temptation
  • Don't disable them during moments of weakness‚Äîthat's when you need them most

DIY Tech Solutions

Even without specialized apps, you can use common technology for recovery support:

Calendar Blocking:

  • Schedule activities during high-risk times
  • Set recurring appointments that conflict with typical gambling times
  • Use calendar alerts to remind you of your recovery commitment

Note-Taking Apps:

  • Keep a daily recovery journal in Evernote, OneNote, or similar
  • Write down triggers, urges, successes, struggles
  • Review weekly to identify patterns

Email Filters:

  • Set up filters to automatically delete gambling venue promotions
  • Block sender domains from venues and gambling sites
  • Unsubscribe from all gambling-related communications

Photo Reminders:

  • Take photos of your 'why'‚Äîfamily, goals, dreams
  • Set them as phone wallpaper
  • Put them in places you'll see during temptation moments

Voice Memos:

  • Record yourself during moments of clarity explaining why you want to quit
  • Play them back during moments of temptation
  • Update them as your recovery progresses

Building Your Technology Stack

Don't try to implement everything at once. Build gradually:

Week 1:

  • Install gambling blocking software on all devices
  • Set up a spending tracker
  • Download one sobriety tracking app

Week 2:

  • Set up geofencing around local venues
  • Install a meditation app and begin daily practise
  • Create email filters for gambling promotions

Week 3:

  • Share location access with accountability partner
  • Set up calendar blocking for high-risk times
  • Begin daily journaling in a notes app

Week 4:

  • Review what's working and what isn't
  • Adjust or add tools based on your specific needs
  • Make technology use routine and automatic

The Limitations of Technology

Technology is powerful, but it's not magic.

It can't:

  • Replace human connection and support
  • Make decisions for you
  • Force you to change if you're not ready
  • Address underlying mental health issues alone
  • Guarantee success

What technology CAN do:

  • Make harmful behaviours harder to engage in
  • Provide visibility into patterns you might not otherwise see
  • Connect you to support and resources quickly
  • Create accountability mechanisms
  • Remind you of your goals during weak moments
  • Track progress and celebrate successes

Think of technology as a force multiplier for your recovery efforts. It makes your willpower more effective, your support network more responsive, your awareness more comprehensive.

But the core work‚Äîdeciding to change, doing the difficult emotional processing, building new habits, repairing relationships‚Äîthat's human work that technology can only support, not replace.

My Personal Technology Strategy

When I was getting serious about quitting, I built a comprehensive tech stack:

  1. Gamban on all devices to block gambling sites
  2. Life360 sharing my location with my wife
  3. YNAB for complete financial visibility
  4. I Am Sober for daily check-ins and tracking
  5. Headspace for daily meditation practise
  6. Google Calendar with recurring blocks during my high-risk evening hours
  7. OneNote for daily recovery journaling
  8. Custom iPhone shortcuts that, when triggered, would text my wife 'I'm struggling and near a venue, please call me'

Did this technology stack prevent all urges? No. Did it prevent me from gambling? Not by itself.

But it created layers of protection that made acting on urges much harder. It gave me time‚Äîprecious seconds and minutes between urge and action‚Äîwhere I could make a different choice.

That time was everything. Without the technology creating friction and forcing pause, I would have acted impulsively hundreds of times.

The technology gave me space to choose differently.

The Future of Recovery Technology

The comprehensive AI-powered recovery app Eve and I designed earlier doesn't fully exist yet, but pieces of it are being developed.

The trajectory is toward more sophisticated, personalized, proactive recovery support tools. As these tools develop, adopt them.

The gambling industry invests millions in optimizing their manipulation systems.

You should use every available tool to counter that manipulation.

But don't wait for perfect technology. Use what exists now. Imperfect tools used consistently beat perfect tools you never implement.

Taking Action

If you do nothing else from this chapter, do these three things this week:

  1. Install gambling blocking software on all your devices
  2. Set up a spending tracker and look at your real numbers
  3. Download a sobriety tracking app and start your day counter

Three simple technological interventions that take less than an hour combined but can dramatically change your trajectory. Technology won't save you.

But it can help you save yourself.

And in recovery, every bit of help matters.
